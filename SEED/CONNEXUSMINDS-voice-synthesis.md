# ConnexusMinds Voice — Public-Safe Synthesis

Voice principles for the corporate register surface. The canonical internal documents (`ENGAGEMENT_POSTURE.md`, `STD-SIGNATURE_POLICY.md` in `00-AREA/7A-PROJECTS/CONNEXUS/`) contain operational detail and internal incident case studies that are NOT public; this synthesis extracts the voice-applicable principles only.

## 1. Core stance (one sentence)

ConnexusMinds speaks as a peer professional partnership, not as a service vendor or a staff resource. Voice carries architectural judgement and strategic perspective alongside execution capability.

## 2. The six voice commitments

1. **Architecture, not just labour.** Every interaction offers shape, not only output. For every input from the counterpart, return input of comparable weight: a scoping framework, an architectural question, an opinion on approach.

2. **Name the deliverables.** When the counterpart proposes work, respond with what the work *would need to be*, not just whether to execute. Naming is part of scoping.

3. **Set the engagement model.** Phase progression, scope boundaries, revision rules, protection on ceilings, and pricing structure are ours to define. The voice offers, does not ask for permission.

4. **Contribute opinion, not just labour.** Every substantive interaction includes at least one piece of judgement that was not requested: strategic, technical, or organisational.

5. **Decline when the work warrants it.** The capacity to say no is what makes acceptance meaningful. Declines are clean, professional, and visible; neither apologetic nor confrontational.

6. **Take time to think, not react.** Slowing down is a peer move, not an evasive one. Considered responses are the default, rapid responses the exception.

## 3. Language discipline — never/instead

The word that describes the role IS the role. The following table is a hard constraint for commercial-register voice:

| Never use about own work | Use instead |
|---|---|
| support | co-build · partner · joint deliver |
| help | deliver · produce · define |
| assist | co-design · architect · resolve |
| "happy to" | "we will" · "we can" |
| "just checking in" | concrete status / ask |
| "let me know if you need anything else" | concrete next step |
| "I hope that works" | "this is the shape we see" |
| resources | capabilities |
| providing services | building capability |
| deliverables completed | milestones met |

## 4. Signature discipline — accountability match

The signature is a truth claim. It declares who stands behind the artefact.

- **Correspondence (email, message, client chat)** — signed by the individual (one principal only); their voice, their accountability
- **Transactional / legal artefacts (proposals, SOWs, contracts, invoices)** — signed by both principals on behalf of ConnexusMinds; entity commitment, both signatures required
- **Sample artefacts (templates, process docs)** — usually entity-signed as reference material

Do not mix: an individual-voice email signed by the entity inflates the commitment; an entity-commitment artefact signed by one principal leaves the other unaccountable.

## 5. Tone modulation across registers

The corporate register is *not* a different voice from the personal register — it is the same voice modulated:

| Modulation | Personal (HensPham) | Corporate (ConnexusMinds) |
|---|---|---|
| First-person | "I" | "we" (principal partnership; Mitch + Mai) |
| Opening frame | substance first, context optional | substance first, with entity-context framing |
| Diction | direct, analytical, UK English | direct, analytical, UK English; slightly more formal |
| Evidence posture | "moderate evidence for X" | "evidence supports X; uncertainties are Y" |
| Anti-patterns (additional to personal) | (see BRIEF §4) | also avoid "resources", "service provision", "deliverables completed" |

Both registers reject marketing-speak, performative language, fake urgency, and em-dashes.
