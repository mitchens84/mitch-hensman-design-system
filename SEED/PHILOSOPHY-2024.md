# Design Philosophy — December 2024

Eight governing principles. Each derives from Mitch's LBS philosophy framework. Documented 2024-12; archived but never superseded. Claude Design authoring should treat this as mature starting material — keep, revise, or replace with rationale captured in `decisions/`.

## Principle 1: Modular Architecture

**LBS source**: MINIMALISM, RESPONSIBILITY_SPHERE

**Statement**: Design the website as a system of independent, interconnected modules that can evolve without disrupting the whole.

**Rationale**: Engineering principles suggest loosely coupled systems are more maintainable and adaptable than monolithic structures. Focus design effort on components within immediate control while allowing future expansion.

**Implementation**:
- Section-based layouts where professional services, personal content, and creative work operate as distinct modules
- Shared design language (typography, colour, spacing) that unifies without constraining
- Content management enabling additions and modifications without redesign
- Clear information boundaries preventing one content type from overwhelming others

**Anti-patterns**:
- Rigid templates requiring full redesign for new content types
- Interdependencies where changes cascade unpredictably
- Over-engineering for hypothetical future needs

## Principle 2: Authentic Coherence

**LBS source**: FUTURE_SELF, STATUS_GAMES resistance

**Statement**: Align internal identity with external presentation, creating consistency across all content areas while resisting performative design choices.

**Rationale**: A website should genuinely reflect who you are, not who you think visitors want you to be. Substance over appearance.

**Implementation**:
- Consistent voice and values across professional and personal sections
- Design aesthetic reflecting genuine preferences, not industry trends
- Transparency about expertise boundaries and service limitations
- Personal elements integrated rather than hidden
- Avoiding visual flourishes that signal sophistication without delivering value

**Anti-patterns**:
- Separate "personas" for different audiences that feel disconnected
- Copying competitor aesthetics to appear more established
- Hiding personal content to seem more "professional"
- Overstating capabilities or experience

## Principle 3: Progressive Value Delivery

**LBS source**: TEACHING, CAMPSITE_RULE

**Statement**: Structure content so visitors receive genuine value at every engagement level, from surface browsing to deep exploration.

**Rationale**: TEACHING centres on sharing knowledge freely. CAMPSITE_RULE demands leaving visitors better than you found them. Deliver value regardless of engagement depth.

**Implementation**:
- Three-tier information architecture:
  - Essential: immediate orientation — who you are, what you offer, how to engage
  - Detailed: full context — case studies, methodology, extended insights
  - Expert: deep reference — technical appendices, frameworks, raw resources
- Standalone value at each tier (no "teaser" content requiring deeper engagement)
- Clear navigation enabling visitors to self-select appropriate depth
- Free resources and insights that genuinely help, regardless of commercial relationship

**Anti-patterns**:
- Content that only makes sense after extensive exploration
- Gated resources requiring email capture for basic information
- Vague service descriptions requiring contact to understand offerings
- "Read more" links leading to minimal additional value

## Principle 4: Causality-Aware Design

**LBS source**: CAUSALITY, ALTRUISM

**Statement**: Consider upstream and downstream impacts of every design decision on visitors, systems, and environment.

**Rationale**: Expand design consideration set beyond immediate aesthetics to include accessibility, performance, environmental impact, and visitor wellbeing.

**Implementation**:
- Accessibility standards (WCAG AA minimum) ensuring usability across abilities
- Performance optimisation reducing loading times and data consumption
- Mobile-first responsive design recognising diverse device contexts
- Dark mode option reducing eye strain and energy consumption
- Clear typography and sufficient contrast for readability
- Honest representation of services preventing mismatched expectations

**Anti-patterns**:
- Heavy animations or images that slow loading without proportionate value
- Design choices that exclude users with disabilities
- Mobile-hostile layouts requiring desktop access
- Misleading calls-to-action creating false expectations

## Principle 5: Cognitive Efficiency

**LBS source**: REASON_EXPLANATION, MINIMALISM

**Statement**: Optimise for visitor comprehension and decision-making, minimising cognitive load while maximising information transfer.

**Rationale**: Clear thinking and good explanations over emotional appeal. Resource efficiency, including cognitive resources.

**Implementation**:
- Logical navigation reflecting visitor mental models
- Consistent interaction patterns reducing learning curve
- Visual hierarchy guiding attention to important elements
- White space providing cognitive breathing room
- Clear calls-to-action with explicit outcomes
- Minimal choices at each decision point (Hick's Law application)

**Anti-patterns**:
- Navigation structures requiring trial-and-error exploration
- Inconsistent button styles or interaction patterns
- Dense layouts without visual hierarchy
- Ambiguous calls-to-action ("Learn more" without context)
- Decision paralysis from too many simultaneous options

## Principle 6: Graceful Evolution

**LBS source**: RADICAL_ACCEPTANCE, HABITS

**Statement**: Design for change, accepting impermanence and creating structures that support sustainable content maintenance.

**Rationale**: The website will change as services, interests, and circumstances evolve. Make ongoing maintenance natural rather than burdensome.

**Implementation**:
- Content management enabling updates without developer intervention
- Archival strategy for outdated content (retire gracefully, don't delete history)
- Version-friendly design allowing iterative improvement
- Maintenance-friendly technology choices within capability boundaries
- Documentation of design decisions enabling future modifications
- Regular review cadence built into workflow

**Anti-patterns**:
- Complex technology stacks requiring specialist maintenance
- "Launch and forget" approach leading to stale content
- Perfectionism preventing incremental improvements
- Undocumented design decisions blocking future changes

---

## Principle 7: Audience Pathway Clarity

**LBS source**: DECISION_MAKING, FREETHINKING

**Statement**: Create distinct, clearly signposted pathways for different visitor types without fragmenting the unified brand experience.

**Rationale**: Different visitors have different needs; design should acknowledge diversity while maintaining coherent identity.

**Implementation**:
- Clear entry points for primary audience types:
  - Professional services seekers → Service offerings, case studies, contact
  - Personal content explorers → Insights, creative work, philosophy
  - Potential collaborators → Background, approach, values
- Permeable boundaries allowing cross-exploration
- Consistent design language across pathways
- Self-selection mechanisms respecting visitor autonomy

**Anti-patterns**:
- Forcing all visitors through single funnel regardless of intent
- Completely separate "sub-sites" that feel disconnected
- Assumptions about visitor intent without providing alternatives
- Hidden content requiring insider knowledge to discover

---

## Principle 8: Relationship Invitation

**LBS source**: ALTRUISM, CAMPSITE_RULE

**Statement**: Design interactions that invite genuine connection rather than extract engagement, prioritising visitor autonomy and long-term relationship over immediate conversion.

**Rationale**: Invite relationship without manipulation. Respect visitor autonomy while creating clear pathways for those who want to engage further.

**Implementation**:
- Multiple contact pathways matching different comfort levels (email, social, scheduling)
- Newsletter or updates as genuine value exchange, not lead capture
- Clear service boundaries preventing scope confusion
- Respectful follow-up expectations set upfront
- Social proof through genuine testimonials and case studies, not vanity metrics
- Exit paths as smooth as entry paths (no dark patterns)

**Anti-patterns**:
- Pop-ups and interruptions prioritising capture over experience
- Email capture walls blocking content access
- Fake urgency or scarcity tactics
- Vanity metrics (follower counts, client logos) without substantive value
- Aggressive retargeting creating surveillance feeling

## Application Matrix

| Principle | Professional Services | Personal Content | Creative Work |
|---|---|---|---|
| Modular Architecture | Service modules | Blog/insights system | Portfolio galleries |
| Authentic Coherence | Genuine expertise boundaries | Unfiltered perspective | True creative voice |
| Progressive Value | Service → Case study → Contact | Insight → Deep dive → Discussion | Piece → Process → Collection |
| Causality-Aware | Clear expectations | Accessible writing | Alt text, descriptions |
| Cognitive Efficiency | Service clarity | Scannable format | Visual hierarchy |
| Graceful Evolution | Service updates | Content archival | Portfolio curation |
| Audience Pathways | Client journey | Reader journey | Appreciator journey |
| Relationship Invitation | Consultation pathway | Discussion invitation | Collaboration opening |

## Quality Gate

Before any design decision, verify:
- Which principle(s) does this serve?
- Does this create value for visitors or primarily for the site owner?
- Can this be maintained sustainably?
- Does this respect visitor autonomy and intelligence?
- Would this choice survive removal of all competitors' influence?
- Does this align with authentic self-presentation?
